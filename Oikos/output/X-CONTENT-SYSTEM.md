## 4. REPLY STRATEGY

### The 30-Minute Reply Rule
The X algorithm awards 75 points per author-reply-to-reply vs. 0.5 per like. That means the 30 minutes after every post are the highest-leverage activity in this entire GTM system. When Daniel posts, he must be available to reply for the next 60 minutes minimum.

### Reply Playbook by Comment Type

**Type 1: Genuine question about mechanics**
Response: Answer in full, then add "Thread below goes deeper:" + link to relevant tweet in the thread.
Goal: Keep the conversation going, increase reply depth, signal expertise.
Example: Q: "How does the floor price actually go up?" A: "Great question. When the Discovery range gets exhausted by buying pressure, the protocol triggers a shift event: funds move from Discovery into Floor Range, permanently raising the minimum buy-back price. It's automatic. No human involved. Here's the mechanic in the thread above 👆"

**Type 2: Skepticism / FUD ("This sounds like a rug")**
Response: Welcome it. Never defensive. Provide specifics.
Template: "Healthy skepticism — you should ask this. [Specific answer]. The audit report is public: [first reply link]. Read it. If you find something that doesn't add up, reply here and I'll address it."
Goal: Convert skeptics into advocates by treating their questions as legitimate.

**Type 3: Comparison challenge ("Why not just use PumpFun?")**
Response: Agree on PumpFun's success, then pivot to specific differentiator.
Template: "PumpFun is phenomenal for what it does. If you want speed and meme liquidity on Solana, it's the right tool. Oikos is built for creators who want [specific thing]. Different jobs."
Goal: Avoid defensive positioning. Agree then differentiate.

**Type 4: Price question ("When moon / What's OKS price?")**
Response: Redirect without dismissing.
Template: "Not leading with price — OKS is near-illiquid right now and that's not what this phase is about. What we're focused on: [current milestone]. When you understand the mechanics, the price question answers itself."
Goal: Redirect to protocol value, never discuss price targets.

**Type 5: Troll / low-effort**
Response: Ignore. Do not engage. Do not quote-tweet. Scroll past.
Goal: Protect reply thread signal quality. Low-quality engagement drags algorithmic score.

**Type 6: Builder inquiry ("I want to launch a token")**
Response: Immediate DM redirect + Telegram invite.
Template: "This is exactly who we're building for. DM me or join Telegram [link] — I'll give you a walkthrough of what launch looks like."
Goal: Convert X engagement into Telegram funnel.

### Conversation Depth Playbook
Goal: Each post should generate at least 1 reply chain of 3+ exchanges.

Setup: Every post ends with a direct question. Not rhetorical — a specific, answerable question.
Examples:
- "What would you build if your token paid you every time it grew?"
- "What metric would make you trust a new DeFi launchpad?"
- "Name another DeFi protocol with no-liquidation loans. I'll wait."
- "What confuses you most about elastic supply?"

On each response: Reply, then ask a follow-up. Keep the chain alive.
Second reply: "Good point — have you seen [related thing]? Curious if that changes your take."
Third reply: "This is exactly the kind of question that shapes what we document next. I'm going to write a thread about this next week."

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Never say "GM" or "GN" — low-value, algorithmic dead weight
- Never repost memes or random crypto takes — off-brand, destroys signal
- Never announce partnerships or tokens you cannot confirm — legal and trust risk
- Never like your own posts — waste of attention
- Never use ALL CAPS in main body — reads as shouting, algorithmic penalty unclear but community perception negative
- Never post price or "to the moon" content — legal constraint, credibility risk
