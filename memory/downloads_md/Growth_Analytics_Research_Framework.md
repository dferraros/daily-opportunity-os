## Part 1: The Decision Framework

### What CEOs Actually Want to Know

Based on industry best practices, crypto exchange leadership makes weekly decisions around these 5 questions:

| Decision | Required Data | Benchmark |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| **1. Is acquisition working?** | ROAS, CAC, volume per user | ROAS > 4:1 is good, >5:1 excellent |
| **2. Are we acquiring the RIGHT users?** | B2C/B2B split, revenue per segment | Institutional drives 60-80% of volume |
| **3. Is paid bringing NEW revenue or cannibalizing?** | First Purchase vs Life Cycle split | New acquisition should be >40% of paid revenue |
| **4. Where should we invest next?** | Performance by region, channel | Concentrate on 2-3 winning geos |
| **5. Are we trending up or down?** | WoW, MoM delta | Positive trajectory required to scale |

### The €25K/Week Decision Tree

```
START: Should we invest €25K/week in Paid?
        │
        ▼
   ROAS > 2:1? ──No──► DON'T SCALE (optimize first)
        │
       Yes
        ▼
   CAC < LTV/3? ──No──► CAUTIOUS (improve targeting)
        │
       Yes
        ▼
   >40% from New Users? ──No──► RE-EVALUATE (might be cannibalizing)
        │
       Yes
        ▼
   Positive WoW trend? ──No──► HOLD (market conditions)
        │
       Yes
        ▼
   SCALE with confidence
```

## Part 2: Industry Benchmarks

### Key Performance Indicators (Crypto Exchange Industry)

#### CAC Benchmarks
| Segment | CAC Range | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **B2C Retail** | $50-100 | Coinbase historically achieved $5-15 CAC through organic/referral |
| **B2B Institutional** | $250-500 | Higher CAC acceptable due to higher LTV |
| **Blended Average** | $100-150 | Varies significantly by market maturity |

*Source: Coinbase S-1 (2021) reported CAC of $5.15; industry average has increased significantly since.*

#### LTV:CAC Ratio Targets
| Health Level | Ratio | Interpretation |
|--------------|-------|----------------|
| Excellent | >5:1 | Can invest aggressively |
| Good | 3:1 - 5:1 | Healthy, scale carefully |
| Acceptable | 2:1 - 3:1 | Monitor closely |
| Concerning | <2:1 | Optimize before scaling |

*Industry standard target: 3:1 minimum*

#### ROAS Benchmarks
| Performance | ROAS | Action |
|-------------|------|--------|
| Excellent | >5:1 | Scale aggressively |
| Good | 4:1 - 5:1 | Scale with monitoring |
| Acceptable | 2:1 - 4:1 | Optimize, test, iterate |
| Poor | <2:1 | Pause and diagnose |

*For Web3/Crypto specifically: ROAS >4x is considered good, >5x highly effective*

#### Revenue Split Benchmarks (Exchange Industry)
| Source | % of Revenue | Notes |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Trading Fees | 70-85% | Primary revenue driver |
| Staking/Earn | 5-15% | Growing segment |
| Subscription/Premium | 3-8% | Institutional focused |
| Other (Cards, API) | 2-5% | Diversification play |

#### User Segmentation (Chainalysis Framework)
| Segment | Holdings | Behavior |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Retail | <$10K | High volume, high churn |
| Professional | $10K-$10M | Medium volume, sticky |
| Institutional | >$10M | Low volume accounts, high LTV |

*Key insight: Retail wallets are majority by count but minority by value transferred*

## Part 4: Metrics Definitions

### Revenue Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Formula |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Revenue** | Gross trading fees + spread | Sum of all fees collected |
| **ROAS (Nuevos)** | Return on ad spend for new customers only | Revenue from FP users / Ad Spend |
| **ROAS (Total)** | Return including life cycle revenue | Total attributed revenue / Ad Spend |
| **CAC** | Cost to acquire one customer | Total acquisition cost / New customers |
| **LTV** | Lifetime value | (Avg monthly revenue × Gross margin) / Churn rate |
| **LTV:CAC** | Efficiency ratio | LTV / CAC (target: >3:1) |

### Funnel Metrics

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| **Registros** | Users who created an account |
| **Verificados** | Users who completed KYC |
| **First Purchase (FP)** | Users who made first transaction |
| **Monthly Active Users** | Users with ≥1 transaction in period |

### Revenue Attribution

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **First Purchase Revenue** | Revenue from user's first ever transaction |
| **Life Cycle Revenue** | Revenue from repeat transactions (existing users) |
| **New Customer Revenue** | All revenue from users acquired in current period |
| **Existing Customer Revenue** | Revenue from users acquired in previous periods |

## Part 5: What Binance & Coinbase Do

### Binance's Approach
1. **Acquisition by Market**: Heavy focus on regional targeting (APAC, LATAM, MENA)
2. **VIP Program**: Tiered users contribute 35%+ of volume
3. **Launchpool Cadence**: Predictable events drive registration spikes
4. **Referral 2.0**: Up to 50% commission, quality-based tiers
5. **Campaign Benchmark**: $25K spend → 4,600 users → $494K volume → 19.78x ROAS (APAC campaign)

### Coinbase's Approach
1. **Organic First**: 90%+ organic acquisition historically
2. **Education as Acquisition**: Coinbase Earn distributed $2B+ in crypto
3. **Cultural Marketing**: Brand campaigns (Super Bowl, UK "Everything is Fine")
4. **Institutional Pivot**: 60-80% of volume now institutional
5. **Referral Program**: Generates ~10% of new user acquisitions

### Key Takeaways for Bit2Me
1. **Segment aggressively**: B2C vs B2B have completely different economics
2. **Track FP vs LC**: This is the single most important split for acquisition decisions
3. **Regional focus**: Don't spread thin—concentrate on winning markets
4. **Quality over quantity**: ROAS matters more than raw registrations
5. **Delta is everything**: Trends tell the story, not snapshots
