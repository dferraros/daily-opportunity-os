# Feature Landscape — Bit2Me @ Paris Blockchain Week 2026

**Domain:** Crypto exchange event go-to-market (PBW booth + campaigns + B2B)
**Researched:** 2026-03-26
**Event dates:** April 15–16, 2026 — Carrousel du Louvre, Paris

## Event Context: What PBW Actually Is

Paris Blockchain Week is not a retail crypto festival. It is Europe's most institutionally dense digital assets conference. In 2025: 10,000+ attendees, 70%+ C-suite, 36,000 networking meetings, 450+ journalists, €999–€3,699 ticket prices. The 2026 theme is "Where Institutions and Digital Assets Finally Meet." Macron is the first sitting G7 president to address a digital assets conference in history — at PBW 2026.

This context determines every activation decision below. The primary audience is professional, capital-allocating, and regulatory-aware. Retail casual crypto users are structurally excluded by ticket price.

### What This Means for Bit2Me

PBW is not a mass user acquisition event. The €999+ ticket price filters out casual retail. The activation strategy must be dual-track from day one:

- **B2C track**: Capture the crypto-native professional who is also a personal trader. They are qualified potential users. Hook: friction-free sign-up with an event-exclusive offer.
- **B2B track**: Target institutional executives, fintechs, and banks for partnership conversations around Bit2Me's white-label API, custody integrations, and MiCA-compliant infrastructure.

These two tracks need different booth zones, different collateral, and different follow-up sequences.

### How Competitors Position Themselves

**Bybit EU (Lead Sponsor — dominant):**
MiCA compliance as legitimacy signal. "Beyond trading platform, into full financial platform." CEO on main stage captures the institutional narrative. Their playbook: regulatory authorization + EU passport + main stage credibility. Lead sponsorship budget: estimated €150k–€300k. They are playing the same MiCA card Bit2Me holds — but Bit2Me holds it first (Spain's CNMV authorization came before Bybit EU's).

**Kraken:**
Compliance-first brand story, not product-first. "Exchanges without regulatory infrastructure can only scale so far." Institutional credibility over retail flashiness. Running the Kraken Institutional Forum 2026 as a parallel activation. Their differentiation is earned regulatory trust, not volume.

**Crypto.com:**
Pure consumer brand play. Rewards ecosystem, lifestyle aesthetics, sponsorship of sports properties. Three consecutive years at PBW = brand recall from consistency, not depth of message. Less institutional, less relevant as B2B competition.

**Coinbase:**
Rarely boots at European events but sends executive representation. Brand trust is the primary asset: 70% of surveyed users cite "trustworthiness" as the reason they chose Coinbase. Regulatory clarity messaging. Sets the credibility benchmark that all other exchanges are measured against.

### The Positioning Gap Bit2Me Can Own

All major competitors position around one of two axes:
1. **Global scale plus compliance** (Bybit EU, Kraken, Coinbase)
2. **Consumer brand plus volume** (Crypto.com, KuCoin)

None of them own: "The regulated European exchange built by and for the Spanish-speaking and Southern European market, backed by the actual banks."

Bit2Me's structural facts as of April 2026 that no competitor can replicate at PBW:
- First Spanish-speaking fintech CNMV-authorized under MiCA — Spain's first-mover advantage
- MiCA passport covering all 27 EU member states, enabling legal operation across the bloc
- Backed by Bankinter, BBVA, Unicaja, Cecabank, and Tether — uniquely bank-integrated in a way no other exchange at the event can claim
- 5.3 billion EUR trading volume in 2025 (8x growth since 2023)
- B2B revenue growing from 18% to 27% of total: bank API infrastructure, not just retail
- Processes Interpol, Europol, and Spanish police seized crypto — institutional trust signal unlike any competitor

No other exchange at PBW can say: "We are Spain's largest exchange, MiCA-authorized first, backed by top Spanish banks, and we handle Europol's seized crypto." That is a full institutional trust stack that Bybit EU, Kraken, and Crypto.com cannot replicate in the European market.

## Anti-Features

What to explicitly avoid at PBW, and why.

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Generic "trade crypto on Bit2Me" messaging | Every exchange says this. Invisible in the Bybit/Kraken/Coinbase noise. At PBW, undifferentiated messaging is worse than no messaging because it wastes conversational opportunities. | Lead with "Spain's first MiCA-authorized exchange. Backed by Bankinter and BBVA." — concrete and verifiable differentiation. |
| Discount wars framed as retail promotions | "0% fees for 30 days" positions Bit2Me as a volume-chasing consumer exchange. In an institutional crowd, it signals low-end product, not credibility. | Frame any offer as "institutional-grade access for professionals" or "priority onboarding." Same economics, different framing. |
| Passive badge scanning as the primary lead strategy | Creates a cold contact list, not qualified leads. The average PBW booth visit is 3 minutes. A scanned badge with no context is worthless in a 300-sponsor follow-up environment. | Pre-qualify every conversation with one question: "Are you here as a personal trader or exploring institutional/API integration?" Separate the CRM routing at source. |
| NFT or POAP attendance mechanics | Adds Web3-native friction. PBW's dominant audience is TradFi institutional — most do not have a MetaMask wallet and will not create one at a conference. | Use CleverTap email capture plus a physical premium leave-behind (branded hardware wallet or curated insights report). |
| Sweepstake or raffle as lead magnet | Attracts prize hunters, not buyers. Post-event conversion rate from raffle leads is near zero across crypto events. | Use the actual offer (fee reduction, access upgrade) as the hook — it self-selects people with genuine product interest. |
| B2C and B2B from one undifferentiated booth | Different messages delivered to the same person confuse both audiences. A bank executive seeing "0% fees — sign up now" walks away. A retail trader seeing "institutional API integration" feels the product is not for them. | Assign two distinct physical zones to the booth. B2C: product demo screen, sign-up QR, event offer. B2B: private corner table, appointment scheduling, institutional collateral. |
| Post-event follow-up after 48 hours | Lead quality degrades exponentially after a two-day dense event. By day three, 300 other sponsors have also sent emails. The window is same-day for B2B, within one hour for B2C. | Automate B2C follow-up via CleverTap within 1h of QR scan. Assign B2B manual follow-up as an on-site task for the last hours of each event day. |

## MVP Recommendation for PBW Activation

Given a 2–4 week runway with no speaking slot confirmed:

**Week 1 — Must complete:**
1. Define event offer (the hook everything depends on). Internal decision, no external dependency except Diego approval of copy.
2. Get Diego approval on landing page copy and offer mechanics.
3. Build dedicated event landing page with UTM-tracked QR and conversion-optimized CTA.
4. Launch X/Twitter paid campaign targeting PBW attendee profile.

**Week 2 — Must complete:**
5. B2B target list (20–30 priority companies). Research who is attending from bank, fintech, and VC categories. Begin LinkedIn and email outreach.
6. Booth materials briefed, designed, and sent to print. QR plus NFC cards for booth reps.
7. Build and schedule post-event CleverTap nurture sequence for B2C leads. Pre-load, do not build it on event day.
8. Pre-event email to existing Bit2Me user base (two sends: D-7 and D-2).

**Nice to have if budget and bandwidth allow:**
- Private dinner or side event evening of April 14 or April 15 (D-1 or Day 1 evening). High relationship ROI if 15–20 institutional targets confirmed.
- Bilingual EN/ES landing page variant to lean into the Spanish identity differentiator.
- On-site social content creator assigned specifically to live coverage.

**Defer — not in scope for this sprint:**
- Spanish/LatAm community side event: High strategic potential but requires Paris-based community contacts and 4+ weeks of coordination.
- Live B2B API demo: High conversion value but requires a technical team member on-site for both days. Confirm availability before scoping.
- Full-funnel retargeting campaign: Not enough runway to build a warm audience before April 15.
