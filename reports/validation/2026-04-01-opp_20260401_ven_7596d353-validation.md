## Step 4: Demand Interview Script

**Who to interview:** Venezuelan small retailers (food, clothing, cosmetics, electronics accessories) currently selling via WhatsApp, processing 20-200 orders/week, accepting USDT or Zelle. Monthly revenue equivalent $1K-$15K USD.

**How to find them:**
- Instagram search: #tiendasvenezuela, #tienditavenezuela, #comerciovenezolano, #emprendimientovenezuela, #negociosvenezuela
- Filter: accounts with 200-5000 followers, last post within 7 days, WhatsApp number or "escribeme al WS" in bio
- WhatsApp group discovery: gruposwats.com, join "Caracas Ventas" as a buyer, identify the sellers

**Scheduling method:** DM on Instagram: "Hola [nombre], admiro tu tienda. Tienes 15 minutos esta semana para contarme como manejas tus pedidos? Estoy investigando como funciona el comercio por WhatsApp en Venezuela." No mention of product. Schedule the call on WhatsApp voice call (zero friction for the retailer).

**Target:** 5 interviews completed within 7 days.

---

**The 5 Questions:**

**Q1 — Current workflow:**
"Cuentame exactamente como funciona cuando un cliente te escribe por WhatsApp para hacer un pedido — desde que te llega el mensaje hasta que confirmas el pago. Que haces paso a paso?"
*(Listen for: how many steps, what they write manually, where the process breaks, how they track USDT confirmation.)*

**Q2 — Last incident:**
"Cuando fue la ultima vez que se te perdio un pedido o cometiste un error en el proceso? Que paso exactamente?"
*(Listen for: specific story, dollar amount lost, customer reaction, emotional pain — this becomes the core marketing story.)*

**Q3 — What they have tried:**
"Has probado alguna app o herramienta para organizar mejor tus pedidos? Que paso — por que dejaste de usarla o por que nunca encontraste algo que funcionara?"
*(Listen for: which tools — VendExpress, Vercatalogo, Excel, Notion — and why they failed: price, complexity, no USDT support, felt like "too much work to set up".)*

**Q4 — Cost of the problem:**
"Si calculas el tiempo que le dedicas a gestionar pedidos por WhatsApp a la semana, cuantas horas son? Y cuanto dinero calculas que has perdido este mes por pedidos confundidos, cobros que se perdieron, o clientes que no respondieron despues de pedir?"
*(Make them quantify. Venezuelan retailers are pragmatic — they will give a number. This becomes the ROI anchor in copy.)*

**Q5 — Ideal outcome:**
"Si esto funcionara perfecto — si tuvieras la herramienta ideal — que cambiaria en tu dia a dia? Como se veria tu proceso ideal de tomar pedidos y cobrar?"
*(Listen for the dream, not the feature request. "No tener que escribir lo mismo 50 veces" = the real job to be done.)*

## Step 5: Competitor Weakness Analysis

### Competitor 1: Leadsales (LATAM WhatsApp CRM, $97/month)
**Top complaint (G2/Capterra reviews, 2025-2026):** "Messages don't arrive in the app even though they are in WhatsApp — I lost quotes and payments from clients and support did not respond." Additional complaints: improper billing with no recourse, slow performance and downtime cited as major drawbacks across multiple independent reviews, group-only demos (no personalized onboarding).
**What their pricing penalizes:** Solo retailers and micro-businesses. At $97/month with 3 users minimum, a single-operator tienda doing $3K/month revenue cannot justify it — that is 3%+ of gross revenue for a chat organizer. Price anchored to Mexican/Colombian mid-market SMBs, not Venezuelan informal retailers.
**Who they ignore:** Any operator without a formal sales team, anyone outside the Shopify/HubSpot integration ecosystem, and critically: everyone who needs USDT payment confirmation built into their order flow.

### Competitor 2: Zoko (WhatsApp eCommerce, Shopify-native, $299+/month)
**Top complaint (respond.io review + WANotifier analysis, 2026):** "Once you exceed fair use limits, you are charged additional fees per message — sometimes as high as $0.199 per message. This can quickly become unsustainable." Custom automation flows cost $5.99/flow/month extra. No uptime SLAs published for reliability-dependent retail ops.
**What their pricing penalizes:** High-volume informal retailers and anyone not on Shopify. Venezuelan informal retailers operate entirely outside Shopify — Instagram + WhatsApp is their full stack. Conversation-based billing punishes the exact use case where every order requires 5-10 back-and-forth messages to confirm product, size, quantity, address, and USDT payment screenshot.
**Who they ignore:** Non-Shopify sellers (the entire Venezuelan informal retail market). USDT/crypto payments not handled at all.

**Wedge statement:**
"We win with Venezuelan solo retailers doing $1K-$15K/month via WhatsApp who are burned by Leadsales' $97/month price tag and non-existent support, and who Zoko ignores entirely because they don't use Shopify."

## Kill Conditions

What signals within 7 days would kill this opportunity immediately:

1. **WTP ceiling below $9/month confirmed by 7+ out of 10 interviews:** If all interviewed retailers anchor below $5/month without prompting and reject the $9 tier even with a 14-day free trial — Venezuela's WTP ceiling is too low for sustainable SaaS unit economics at this stage. (Current data suggests $10-20/month is possible for ops tools; this validates or kills that assumption.)

2. **Existing tools already adopted and rated "good enough":** If 6 out of 10 interviewed retailers are already actively paying for VendExpress, Whataform, or another local solution and report no major pain with it — the market is more served than signals suggest and switching cost is not justified without a significant differentiation investment.

3. **WhatsApp Business API verification blocked for Venezuelan numbers:** If technical outreach reveals that getting a WhatsApp Business API number verified for +58 Venezuelan phone numbers is blocked or severely rate-limited due to OFAC/Meta compliance restrictions — the technical foundation of the product (API-based automation) is unreachable without a carrier workaround that collapses the speed-to-MVP advantage entirely.
