## SECTION 1 — Day 1 Action: X/Twitter Certification Check (March 29)

X/Twitter requires a valid crypto exchange advertising certification for EU markets before any crypto ad can run. This is a platform-level requirement that cannot be bypassed. Do this first thing on March 29.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Log into X Ads Manager → Account Settings → Certification status
2. **If certified and active:** Note the expiry date. If it expires before April 10, renew now. Proceed to Section 3 (campaign setup).
3. **If expired or missing:** Submit renewal/application at `business.x.com` → Ads → Account certification → Financial Services → Crypto Exchange
4. **Expected timeline:** 2-7 business days. Submitted March 29 = approval arrives April 3-4 (within the Phase 2 window)
5. **While certification is pending:** Complete all other campaign setup (targeting, creatives, copy loading). Set campaign to "Paused" — flip to "Active" the moment certification clears and Diego approves
6. **If certification is blocked entirely (denied or prolonged):** X/Twitter track pauses. Redirect the full EUR 1,500-2,000 budget to Google Ads (if certified) or organic social amplification. Do not wait — redirect immediately.

**Certification does NOT cover:** DeFi ads, NFT ads, token sales. It covers exchange accounts + spot trading promotion only. The PBW campaign uses spot trading messaging — this is in scope.

**Important:** Belgium is an excluded market for X crypto ads. Do not target Belgium in any campaign (this is enforced in targeting specs in Section 4).

## SECTION 2 — Day 1 Action: Google Ads Certification Check (March 29)

Google requires a separate crypto exchange certification for EU markets. The decision logic is simple:

**Step-by-step:**

1. Check if Bit2Me holds a current Google Ads financial product and services certification for crypto exchange in EU (contact the paid ads team or check Google Ads account → Tools → Policy Center → Certifications)
2. **If certified for EU:** Proceed to Section 6 (Google campaign setup). Budget: EUR 500
3. **If NOT certified:** Do NOT pursue during this sprint. Google certification takes 2-4 weeks — not viable for a March 29 start with a March 31-April 4 campaign window.
4. **Decision documented:** If uncertified, write "Google: not pursuing — uncertified, timeline incompatible" in the campaign log and move on. X/Twitter is the primary channel and can carry the full load without Google.

**Time box:** Spend a maximum of 20 minutes on this check. It is a yes/no decision with no middle path.

## SECTION 7 — What NOT to Do

These are hard stops. Any of these will trigger ad rejection or legal risk.

| Prohibition | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Do not use "guaranteed" or "returns" language | Instant ad rejection on X and Google |
| Do not target Belgium | Belgium is an excluded market for X crypto ads |
| Do not use celebrity images without explicit written rights | IP/legal risk |
| Do not claim CNMV "endorses" or "recommends" Bit2Me | CNMV is a regulator, not an endorser — this claim is false and illegal |
| Do not launch ads pointing to a page that is not yet live | 404 destination = wasted budget + quality score penalty |
| Do not launch before Diego approval | All 4 ad variants contain the fee offer claim — unauthorized launch = legal exposure |
| Do not use "bonus" or "reward" language | MiCA Article 77 trigger — ad copy must say "fee waiver" not "bonus" |
| Do not target under-18s | Platform rule + MiCA requirements |
| Do not claim to be the "safest" or "best" exchange | Unsubstantiated superlative = prohibited under MiCA advertising standards |
| Do not use DeFi, NFT, or yield-farming language | Outside the scope of Bit2Me's exchange certification |

## SECTION 9 — Performance Tracking

### Check Frequency
- Days 1-3 (April 1-3): Check X Ads Manager every morning
- Days 4-10 (April 4-10): Check every 2 days

### Key Metrics + Thresholds

| Metric | Target | Action if missed |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| CTR | > 1% | If < 0.5% after 48h, pause underperforming variant. Reallocate budget to winner. |
| Cost per click (CPC) | < EUR 2 | If CPC > EUR 5, tighten targeting: remove broad interests, keep follower targeting only |
| Impressions | > 10,000/day | If < 5,000/day, check certification status and bid floor |
| Spend | Matches daily budget | If spend = EUR 0, check certification, billing, and campaign status |
| Conversions on /pbw | Track with UTM | Alvaro confirms conversion event firing in BigQuery |

### Optimization Decisions

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Variant CTR gap > 2x after 3 days | Pause the lower performer, move budget to winner |
| Ad Set 2 outperforms Ad Set 1 by 2x | Shift 20% of budget from Ad Set 1 to Ad Set 2 |
| CPC climbing (> EUR 3) | Remove broadest interest targeting layer from Ad Set 1 |
| Google campaign runs | Track `utm_source=google` separately in BigQuery — do not blend with X metrics |
